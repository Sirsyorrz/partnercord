H1gh Voltage 8/12/2022 10:06 AM
Hey @Partners and @Affiliate, We have been keeping a close eye on your feedback and questions regarding Season 8. While everyone seems to have fun with the new PVP system, some of the issues also surfaced quite quickly after the launch. I'm going to try and answer some of the most burning questions and highlight the steps we are taking to address these problems: Matchmaking: We are improving matchmaking system in general based on the data we've gathered, and also looking into a possibility to allow crews from the same factions queue up against each other. This should widen player pools and improve matchmaking times. The improvements won't happen immediately, but we are aiming at implementing them in January. The system we have in place for SBMM is also being closely monitored. While it works as intended in most cases, if there aren't any perfect matches waiting for your crew when you opt into your next battle, then we do broaden the net of players you could play with over time to ensure you'll always find a match. Rep farming by losing and griefing: We are looking into ways to identify problematic repeated behavior and certain patterns, such as rep grind by losses. While we can't force people to actively engage in a fight or not in an open world sandbox game, where you rely on tools rather than rules, we are thinking of ways to discourage this type of behavior. How exactly? Too early to tell at the moment. For now we are focused on gathering information and thinking of solutions that feel right for everyone. We feel the pain and frustration of PVP-focused players and creators when they get matched with the crew that intentionally sinks within seconds. And we are kindly asking you to try and stay above the toxicity even in such cases. Please be patient and show good sport in both your wins and losses no matter how these were achieved. While we figure out the technical and engineering solutions, something you can help with is encouraging and promoting good-willed competition and PVP etiquette using your platform. If you encounter toxicity or griefing, as always, we're urge you to report the players by raising a ticket - https://support.seaofthieves.com/hc/en-gb/articles/360016890560 Alliances: Since Season 8 is being based in Adventure mode, there will always be a chance of a third-party joining the dance. And this is all supposed to be part of fun and chaotic spirit of Piracy. However, we recognize there is a huge difference between a couple of ships forming an alliance spontaneously/naturally to help each other fight or complete objectives short term, and Alliances that form with a sole purpose of grinding rep with little effort, gaining an unfair advantage and safety in big numbers. Alliance Servers are... tricky and keep being a bit of a headache from our side, since there is no simple change that would magically fix the whole Alliance system and protect it from any exploits whatsoever. Especially if you consider that Sea of Thieves is largely based around player interaction in the first place. We are, again, monitoring the activity and having wider conversations with different teams at the studio. If you encounter players that are abusing the alliance system to gain advantage in PVP - please record and report these cases to us. Gathering more information on this issue is valuable at this moment. Blunderbombs: Changes to the blunderbombs are coming live soon to help ease the frustrations and stop endless spam. Hope this post helps and provides some clarity from our side. We will keep you updated on the progress. Happy sailing, everyone!

🚫Back on Wednesday🚫 12/10/2023 11:12 AM
Re: the confusion with flags and Sovereigns: When the Sovereigns were accepting Flags previously, it was not intended. Although this inadvertently added some convenience for cashing into Athena’s, it was not an intended effect and as such there were issues that came alongside this because it was a bug and not as designed. As an example, you would earn Servants of the Flame Allegiance when cashing in Flags at the Sovereign, despite it never being intended as a Reaper’s cash in location. We’ve now fixed the situation to how it was originally intended, that you cannot cash in Flags at the Sovereigns. However, as always intended Guilds adds this convenience as part of being a Guild Emissary. When you are a Guild Emissary, Non-Battle Flags (e.g. a Gold Hoarders Flag) will always be sold to the Reaper’s through the Sovereign. When you are a Guild Emissary Battle Flags (Reaper’s Bones Flags, Athenas Fortune Flag Won in Battle), have now been implemented with the proper logic to ensure your Flags go to the right place. When using a Guild Emissary, the Sovereigns will send Battle Flags to the Reaper’s Bones if you have no faction or are aligned to the Servants, and to Athena’s Fortune if you are aligned to Guardians. They will also send other Trading Company Flags to the Reapers. We also award Athena’s Fortune Reputation if anyone on your crew has bought an Athena’s Fortune Emissary Flag – this is because you only earn Athena’s Fortune Reputation for selling Flags if you are an Athena’s Fortune Emissary – we ensure you don’t miss out on this if you are a Guild Emissary. We also ensure that if you are a Guild Emissary you don’t miss out on the Reputation you would earn as a Reaper or Athena’s Emissary when winning a battle and cashing in the Hourglass. If anyone on your crew has bought an Athena’s Fortune Emissary Flag, when fighting for Guardians you will still get Athena’s Fortune Reputation for Wins and selling the Hourglass. If anyone on your crew has bought a Reaper’s Bones Emissary Flag, when fighting for Servants you will still get Reaper's Bones Reputation for Wins and selling the Hourglass.

@Partners @Partner Legends @Affiliate Using Crosshair Overlays While the use of Third-Party Tools is against the Sea of Thieves Code of Conduct, the use of Software, Monitor features or Physical solutions to overlay a Crosshair on the screen will not be a bannable offence if used for Sea of Thieves. Sea of Thieves, however, does utilise an Anti-Cheat solution alongside other security tools to protect players from malicious users. Therefore, if an external solution providing Crosshair tools does become flagged by one of these security systems, players may find themselves under enforcement for its use. The use of Software, Monitor features or Physical solutions to provide a Crosshair Overlay is therefore fully at your own risk. Tampering with Game Files Tampering with game files is against the Sea of Thieves Code of Conduct, this includes but is not limited to manipulating settings found within the installation folder or adding additional files with the intent to modify the experience. However, it is not a bannable offence for players to use tools to control their graphics driver settings, such as NVIDIA Control Panel, AMD software or Intel Graphics Software. Using ReShade Filters The use of Third-Party Tools to modify the visual experience of Sea of Thieves such as ReShade filters is considered a bannable offence. However, it is not a bannable offence for players to use tools to control their graphics driver settings, such as NVIDIA Control Panel, AMD software or Intel Graphics Software. Website article: https://support.seaofthieves.com/articles/24643308439314-Clarification-regarding-specific-Enforcement-Policies

  • Step 1: Go to https://www.xbox.com/en-US/user/settings/privacy-and-safety
  • Step 2: Under the Others can: section, set the 3rd option to Friends or Blocked (whatever your preference) <---If you have this set to Anyone, they can still get you.
  • Step 3: Open up GameBar with WinKey+G and log out of your account (NOTE: You don't have to uninstall GameBar),
  • Step 4: Ensure that the XBox App is not running in the background. You can end this task in the Task Manager,
  • Step 5: In the in-game Sea of Thieves Settings, go into HUD and turn off Invite Notifications.
  • Step 6: Scramble Gamertags in-game as well
